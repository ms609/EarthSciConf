Abstract submission

Abstracts for posters must be less than 250 words (excluding name and title). Level 4 undergraduates may submit the 350 word abstract prepared for their research dissertations. Abstracts for the Rapid Research competition should just be one sentence long.

Abstracts must be written in 11 pt Calibri font using the following format:

Name: e.g., Other, A.N.

Status: e.g., Level 2 undergraduate

Title: e.g., Volcanic history of the Bahamas

Main text

Abstracts should be submitted using this Word template, e-mailed as an attachment to earthscience.conference@durham.ac.uk by midnight on 20th May 2019.

In the main text of the email, please state if you are submitting for an oral presentation (Level 4 only), for the three minute thesis competition, or for a poster. Undergraduates, please also state if you need help with the costs of poster printing. We will then contact you to discuss options.

If you are submitting an abstract then you do not need to register separately.

In your email, please tell us if you have any dietary preferences for the lunch (e.g. vegetarian/gluten free etc.) and tell us if you are undergrad (plus level)/postgraduate/postdoc/academic staff.

Registration

To attend the conference without submitting an abstract, you must register! To register, send an email to earthscience.conference@durham.ac.uk stating that you would like to attend. This can be done in the same email as abstract submission if you intend to present at the conference (see above).

In the email, please state whether you are undergraduate (plus level), postgraduate, post doctoral, or academic staff and state your department (if outside of Earth Sciences). Please also tell us if you have any dietary preferences for the lunch (e.g. vegetarian/gluten free etc.).

Conference registration includes coffee/biscuits, buffet lunch, drinks reception (during afternoon poster session) and an abstract volume.

Registration closed on 6th May 2019.
